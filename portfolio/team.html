Leadership

ALEX MATHERS

Team Principal

Leads operations, strategy, and performance programs.

Biography and Function
  • Sets the sporting, technical, and commercial direction of AVR with a data‑first ethos.
  • Built cross‑functional “race pods” that pair engineers, strategists, and crew for faster decisions.
  • Champion of marginal gains: targets 1–2% improvements across dozens of micro‑processes.
  • Known for calm crisis management and clear radio discipline during safety‑car chaos.
  • Mentors future leaders and promotes transparent post‑race debriefs with action owners.
  • Spearheaded sustainability goals and supplier partnerships without compromising performance.
  • Ensures drivers’ feedback loops reach aero, chassis, and tyre working groups within 24 hours.
  • Background in mechanical engineering plus an MBA focused on operations excellence.
  • Advocates inclusive team culture, psychological safety, and evidence‑based experiments.
  • Belief: winning is the by‑product of repeatable processes, not one‑off heroics.

PRIYA KAPOOR

Technical Director

Oversees aero, chassis, and PU integration.

Biography and Function
  • Oversees aero, chassis, and power‑unit integration with strict CFD/tunnel/track correlation.
  • Reduced drag while maintaining downforce through iterative sidepod and floor revisions.
  • Drives weekly “design sprints” from ideation to manufacturable solutions within cost caps.
  • Owns upgrade cadence tailored to circuits with distinct aero sensitivity profiles.
  • Instituted telemetry‑driven validation gates before any part reaches FP1.
  • Translates driver handling feedback into setup hypotheses and test plans.
  • Strengthens collaboration across simulation, composites, and manufacturing cells.
  • Champions reliability as performance—fewer DNFs, more points.
  • Promotes skill rotation to grow versatile engineers and resilient teams.
  • Philosophy: correlation isn’t a checkbox; it’s the backbone of development.

MARCUS WU

Head of Race Operations

Manages garage, pit crew, and trackside execution.

Biography and Function
  • Directs garage flow, pit crew choreography, and car turnaround under time pressure.
  • Runs drill cycles that target consistency first, absolute speed second.
  • Designs fail‑safe checklists and redundancy for critical tasks and equipment.
  • Coordinates logistics from freight to spares, minimizing idle inventory and transit risk.
  • Leads “race rehearsal” simulations including red‑flag and multi‑stop contingencies.
  • Aligns pit windows with tyre life predictions in concert with strategy and engineering.
  • Tracks operational KPIs: stop variance, turnaround time, and tooling accuracy.
  • Invests in cross‑training so each role has a competent backup.
  • Keeps garage communications clean: fewer words, clearer intent, faster execution.

Engineering & Pit Crew

SOPHIE LARSON

Lead Aerodynamicist

CFD and wind tunnel correlation lead.

Biography and Function
  • Leads CFD campaigns and wind‑tunnel programs with tight correlation to on‑track results.
  • Targets robust aero across yaw/ride/steer variations, not peak figures alone.
  • Iterates floor edges, fences, and diffuser geometries for predictable balance.
  • Coordinates rakes, pressure taps, and flow‑vis tests to validate hypotheses quickly.
  • Works with chassis to avoid aero wins that compromise mechanical grip.
  • Packages upgrades tailored to circuit characteristics and sensitivity maps.
  • Calibrates models using driver feedback as truth data for future development.
  • Runs post‑event retros on what correlated and what didn’t to drive learning.
  • Ensures designs remain manufacturable and reliable under budget caps.
  • Maxim: stability breeds confidence; confidence breeds lap time.

ENZO RUSSO

Chief Strategist

Race modeling, tyre life, pit windows.

Biography and Function
  • Models stint outcomes and pit windows using probabilistic tools and scenario trees.
  • Assesses undercut/overcut viability against track evolution and traffic patterns.
  • Maintains safety‑car and VSC probability tables tailored to each circuit.
  • Aligns tyre plans with drivers’ degradation curves and compound confidence windows.
  • Runs live deltas vs. competitors, flagging “go/no‑go” moments with clear thresholds.
  • Balances risk and points haul across the season—championship math over single‑race glory.
  • Converts hindsight into playbook rules and pre‑race decision ladders.
  • Minimizes double‑stack losses and box timing errors through ops alignment.
  • Advocates decisive calls: late ambiguity costs more than early conviction.
  • Principle: strategy is choosing which uncertainties you’re willing to own.

NIA CARTER

Crew Chief

Pit stop drills and car turnaround.

Biography and Function
  • Leads pit crew selection, roles, and choreography for sub‑2‑second consistency.
  • Builds muscle memory via micro‑drills: wheel‑gun handoffs, nut indexing, jack timing.
  • Tracks stop variance and addresses outliers with targeted retraining.
  • Ensures equipment health: gun calibration, jack seals, and wheel nut tolerances.
  • Synchronizes crew plans with strategy for late boxes and emergency calls.
  • Runs “stress reps” under noise, heat, and time compression for realism.
  • Promotes safety and ergonomics to reduce fatigue and injury risk.
  • Keeps morale high with clear accountability and reinforcement after clean stops.
  • Designs crew rotations to avoid overuse and keep reaction times sharp.
  • Credo: smooth is fast, and fast is safe when practiced right.

  • Designs quality checks that catch sensor drift before it skews decisions.
  • Converts raw data into actionable race metrics for strategy and engineering.

  • Maintains dashboards with driver‑centric views for quick comprehension on the radio.
